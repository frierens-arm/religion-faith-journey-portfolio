February

Wisdom

"I will use my gift of Wisdom in everything I do."

Opposes Pride
Wisdom — February
What It Means
Definition
Wisdom is having knowledge and actually knowing what to do with it. It is not just being smart. It is making good choices and being honest when you mess up.
St. Augustine
St. Augustine spent half his life looking for truth in the wrong places. Fame. Pleasure. Philosophy. But here is the thing. He admitted he was wrong. That is what makes him wise. Not the fact that he never messed up. The fact that he owned it.
"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom." — Proverbs 9:10
Wisdom — February
My Experience
What I Did
I got into an argument with my parents and I knew it was my fault. I just admitted it instead of keep going. That is harder than it sounds. Most people would just keep arguing. Admitting you are wrong takes guts.
Real Life
My table tennis coach shows me stuff that applies to normal life, not just ping pong. One thing he taught me. Never look at someone and judge them on one small thing. Look at the full picture. That is basically what wisdom is.
Vs. Pride
Pride is when people only look at someone's weak points. If you do that, you will never get anywhere. In sport or in life. Pride makes you think you already know everything. That is the opposite of being wise.
Wisdom — February
Prayer
Lord, help me admit when I am wrong.
Even when it is embarrassing.
Teach me to see the full picture,
not just the parts that are easy to judge.
I do not want to be proud.
I want to be wise.

Amen.

March

Perseverance

"I will stick to the goals I have set for myself."

Opposes Sloth
Perseverance — March
What It Means
Definition
Perseverance is not giving up. Even when things are hard. Even when you are frustrated. You just keep going. That is it. There is no secret trick. You just do not stop.
St. Augustine & Moses
St. Augustine lived a bad life for a long time. But he never stopped looking for God. He kept searching through all the wrong ways until he found the right one. And Moses. He was not a good speaker and Pharaoh kept saying no. But Moses just kept going back. That is perseverance.
"Let us not become weary in doing good, for at the proper time we will reap a harvest if we do not give up." — Galatians 6:9
Perseverance — March
My Experience
What I Did
Year 3 was the worst I have played table tennis in five years. But I did not stop. I kept training. It was frustrating but quitting would have been worse. And when I teach at camps, my patience gets tested a lot. Nothing is working and nobody is improving. But you just keep going.
Real Life
Think about players like Ma Long or Miwa Harimoto. If they quit when they were down 0-3, they would never have come back to win 4-3 and become champions. Champions do not never lose. They just never stop.
Vs. Sloth
Sloth is a real problem. People quit when they do not see results right away. But that is kind of the whole point. You keep going even when it feels like nothing is happening. That is what separates people who make it from people who do not.
Perseverance — March
Prayer
Lord, I want to quit sometimes.
A lot of times actually.
Help me remember why I started.
Even when nothing is working.
Even when I feel like nothing is changing.
I am going to keep going.

Amen.
🙏
April

Reverence

"I will honour and respect God's gift of life."

Opposes Envy
Reverence — April
What It Means
Definition
Reverence is more than just being polite. It is actually respecting someone. Valuing them. Treating them like they matter. Because they do. It sounds simple but most people do not actually do it.
St. Augustine & King David
St. Augustine eventually changed his entire life because he started respecting God. That is reverence. And King David had the chance to kill Saul. Saul was literally trying to kill him. But David spared him because Saul was chosen by God. That is respect even when it does not make sense.
"Show proper respect to everyone." — 1 Peter 2:17
Reverence — April
My Experience
What I Did
I showed respect to my parents and my table tennis coach this month. It is easy to respect someone who is helping you. But my coach gives advice to everyone at the club. Even people who do not like him. I respect that a lot.
Real Life
I respect my dad a lot. He can talk to anyone. Anywhere. Strangers, family, does not matter. He treats everyone the same. That is not easy to do. And I want to be like that.
Vs. Envy
Envy is when people cannot respect others because they are jealous. Jealous of what someone has. Or what they can do. If people just appreciated what they have instead of being jealous of others, things would be a lot better.
Reverence — April
Prayer
Lord, help me treat people with respect.
Not just the people I like.
Everyone.
When I feel jealous, remind me
what I already have.
Help me value the people around me.

Amen.

May

Responsibility

"I will honour my commitments even when it becomes difficult."

Opposes Greed
Responsibility — May
Full Reflection
Definition & Scripture
Responsibility means owning what you do. Not blaming other people. Not making excuses. After St. Augustine found God, he did not just say sorry and move on. He became a bishop and spent the rest of his life helping people. That is taking responsibility. Same with Noah. God told him to build an ark. He did it. No complaining. No giving up.
My Experience
I helped my partners finish our science project this month. It is easy to be responsible when you feel good. It is the moments when you are exhausted and just want to sleep that it actually counts. My dad goes to work every day. Even when he is tired. That is responsibility.
Vs. Greed
Greed is when people only care about themselves. They do not think about how what they do affects other people. If everyone just did their own part, things would actually work. People would trust each other more.
Responsibility — May
Lord, help me be someone people can count on.
When I am tired, give me strength.
Help me own what I do.
Not just the easy stuff.
The hard stuff too.

Amen.
Growing in faith.
